King Krule

Name after King K. Rool, a character in the Donkey Kong series of video games, King Krule is a solo project of London-based Archy Marshall. The singer, songwriter, guitarist, and keyboardist raised eyebrows with the 2010 single "Out Getting Ribs," released under the alias Zoo Kid, which showcased his distinctively raw voice. Anticipation for Marshall's first album grew, as the song earned him a spot on various high-profile "artists to watch" lists, as well as a nomination in BBC's Sound of 2013 poll. In 2013, Marshall released the first King Krule album, 6 Feet Beneath the Moon. Marshall then collaborated with his brother Jack for the relatively fluid, more electronic A New Place 2 Drown.

Beshken

Originally trained as jazz guitarist, Beshken first became interested in dance music while djing house parties and clubs in Los Angeles. Heavily influenced by the city's rich, colorful, and diverse sounds, he has cultured his identity, ideologoy, and musical dexterity with constant artistic and introspective development. Now based in Brooklyn, 20-year-old Ben Shirken creates a lush and effervescent atmosphere of sound by blending elements of electronic music, jazz, and indie rock. Ben's live show blends electronic and organic elements with custom visuals to create a unique experience, one that delves into the subconscious and reflects his curiosity to combine real world sounds with digital elements.

Lewis Del Mar

Not a guy but rather two guys, Lewis Del Mar is a New York based electro-acoustic indie pop duo made up of Washington, D.C. transplants Danny Miller (singer/songwriter) and Max Harwood (drummer/producer). Having collaborated on and off since grade school, they formed the group in 2013 after relocating to Rockaway Beach, and the project after their fathers (both names Lewis) and their costal locale. Melodic and percussion-driven, and incorporating samples and manipulated sound, their sleek debut single, "Loud(y)," went viral in 2015, topping Hype Machine's Popular Tracks chart.

alt-J

English rock outfit alt-J - named for the delta symbol that appears when pressing "alt" and "J" on a Mac keyboard - deliver eccentric indie rock that experiments with rhythm, song structure, percussion, and space. Following the 2012 release of their critically acclaimed, award winning debut, An Awesome Wave, they expanded their scope with 2014's This Is All Yours and 2017's Relaxer.

Foreign Air

Foreign Air released their first single "Free Animal" in 2015 and since their music has been featured in commercials, tv shows, and trailers. They have made appearances at Hangout, FireFly, Shaky Knees, All Things Go festivals as well as toured alongside the likes of Phantogram, BORNS, and Bishop Briggs.
